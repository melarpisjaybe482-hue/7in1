How accurate are these numbers?
These yield and ROI figures are rough estimates for demonstration purposes. They are calculated using:

• Soil health score from your single sensor (S-01)
• National average yield data per crop type (not your specific field)
• Approximate local market prices per kilogram
• Estimated fertilizer cost from the AI recipe

Real accuracy requires: multiple sensors across the field, actual local market price data, crop variety details, irrigation schedule, pest history, and professional agronomist assessment. With only 1 sensor, the soil health score reflects only one point in your field — actual yield may vary significantly.

How much carbon your soil stores and why it matters
🌿
What is Soil Carbon (OC)?
Organic Carbon = the amount of dead plant matter, roots, and microbe material in your soil
Think of Organic Carbon like food for your soil. The more carbon your soil has, the healthier and more fertile it is. Low carbon means metals like aluminum spread faster, nutrients wash away quicker, and crops grow weaker.

💡 What are Carbon Credits? (Simple Explanation)
🌎
The problem: Factories and vehicles release CO₂ gas which causes climate change. Governments and companies are trying to reduce this.
🌿
What your soil does: Soil with high organic carbon absorbs CO₂ from the air and locks it underground. This is called carbon sequestration — your soil is literally cleaning the air.
💰
Carbon credits: Companies that want to "offset" their pollution can pay farmers whose soil stores carbon. Each "carbon credit" = 1 tonne of CO₂ stored. In the global market, 1 credit is worth roughly $10–$50 USD.
⚠️
Important note: The value shown here is an educational estimate. To actually sell carbon credits, a farm needs official certification, lab soil tests, third-party verification, and registration with a carbon registry like Verra or Gold Standard.

🔧 How to increase your soil carbon
🪲 Add compost — Apply 5–10 kg of compost per square meter. Compost breaks down into organic carbon over time.
🌾 Use crop residue — Instead of burning old plant material, leave it on the soil. It decomposes and adds carbon.
🚫 Avoid over-tilling — Turning the soil too much releases carbon back into the air. Minimize digging.
🌿 Plant cover crops — Growing plants like legumes between seasons adds root carbon to the soil.
